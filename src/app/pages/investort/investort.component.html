Investor Type by Term

Short

    Risk level
  • Usually holding for less than a year
  • Timing the market(quick trades for quick profit)
  • Usually riskier, but quick profit if done correctly
  • Usually very volatile,need to have an exit strategy
  • Active investing(need to be constantly checked on)

Medium

    Risk level
  • Usually holding for 1 to 3 years
  • Might change depending goals and risk tolerance but usually reliable
  • Perfect balance between risk and return
  • Usually less volatile but is still good to have an exit strategy
  • This is kind of a passive investment

Long

    Risk level
  • Usually holding till retirement or more than 10 years
  • Time in the market(when done correctly can make good profit)
  • Risk is usually very low but patience is key
  • Small and steady pace can make big returns over the years
  • Passive investing, pretty much just set and forget

You should also be aware of the following:

  • You only lose money if you sell, stocks can't go up forever so if you believe in the company you should hold during difficult times
  • Average down: if your stock is negative due to special circumstances (like COVID) then you can invest more money and when it recovers the profit would be higher
  • Invest in things/companies that you are interested in, this would make the research way easier
  • Don't put all your eggs in one basket, instead your portfolio should be diversified and this would bring down the risk
